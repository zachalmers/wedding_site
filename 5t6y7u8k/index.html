Our story

A fated story - two mid-20-year-olds trying to find themselves and finding each other instead; the right time, right place. A first date at the Willow Room, Zach’s introduction to Bad Bunny (on repeat on a long road trip through the South), a whirlwind year in Miami long-distance during Covid, a surprise return to Chicago for Umangi’s residency. Since then, our love story has traversed the globe, from Guatemala and Sicily, to New Zealand. After many moons of residency, music shows, and countless restaurants, Zach popped the question in Istanbul, Turkey on a boat in the Bosphorus strait. Where the East and West (metaphorically and literally) collide, he proposed with a ring rooted in love, lore, and family.

We’re so excited to have our loved ones with us on this special weekend that celebrates love, family and the cultures that have shaped us! We thank you from the bottom of our hearts for making the effort to celebrate us and our families.

Why Oaxaca

On a serendipitous trip to Oaxaca City, Zach and his now best man Karthik discovered the wealth of culinary yumminess, from moles to mezcal, rich artisanal culture, and the overall sense of place in this historical city. When deciding the wedding location, Umangi came around to the idea and wholly trusted Zach in deciding this city as our wedding destination (and she’s thrilled she did!).

For us, Oaxaca City is a place where our values integrate, with appreciation of culture, cuisine, and community in a beautifully sunny location in the backdrop of the Sierra Madre Mountains. (Also moles, tlayudas, tostadas galore.)

Oaxaca is known for its indigenous culture, 16th century colonial architecture, rich art scene, and is known as one of Mexico’s gastronomic capitals. We hope you enjoy this whimsical city as much as we do!

Practical notes

Weather

Oaxaca has a dry, sunny desert-like climate. Midday high temperatures are around 80 degrees with evening temperatures dipping to 50s. Bring layers, especially shawls and light jackets for the nighttime events. Sunscreen is a must!

Altitude

Oaxaca sits at 5,000 ft in elevation. Take it easy on day one, drink water (not mezcal), and keep snacks handy.

Kids

Kids are welcome. If you need childcare suggestions, please reach out closer to the event.

Safety

Oaxaca is generally safe and welcoming. Use normal city awareness and consider rideshare at night for longer journeys.

Deadlines

Haldi

About

The haldi is a wedding ritual where relatives and friends apply a paste composed of turmeric, sandalwood and other herbs to the bride and groom. The purpose is to rid the couple of buri nazar (evil eye), offer blessings, and to cleanse the couple before the religious ceremony.

Dress

Think bright colors and breezy outdoor vibes. Yellow is traditional and encouraged, but any cheerful colors are welcome.

Sangeet

About

The Sangeet is a pre-wedding party filled with music, dancing, and dance performances from friends and family. Expect lively music, choreographed dances and a packed dance floor.

Dress

Indian attire is encouraged — think lehengas, saris, or festive wear. Men are welcome to wear kurtas or casual suits. Bold colors and statement accessories are all welcome. Ladies are recommended to wear block heels, flats, or sandals for the entirety of this wedding weekend — cobblestone streets are not friendly to stilettos.

Baraat

About

The Baraat is the wedding procession that marks the groom’s arrival to the ceremony. Accompanied by music, dancing, and plenty of energy, the groom’s family and friends parade together to officially kick off the wedding day (and the bride’s family will also join in!).

Dress

Wear ceremony-ready attire that still allows you to move and dance. Indian formalwear or festive Western outfits are both welcome. The Baraat involves walking, standing, and dancing. Ladies are recommended to wear low, block heels or flats — RIP stilettos. Oaxaca is sunny with a bit of a morning desert-chill, so dress accordingly.

Ceremony

About

The Hindu wedding ceremony is a sacred and symbolic celebration of love, commitment, and unity. Rooted in ancient Vedic tradition and aligned with the Muhurat, the ceremony is led by a priest through a series of pujas, prayers, and vows. These traditions symbolize the spiritual merging of two souls, to journey together through the ups and downs of life. Guests are invited to observe, reflect, and celebrate alongside the couple.

Dress

This is a formal and traditional event. Indian attire is encouraged — saris, lehengas, kurtas are all welcome. Please plan for an outdoor setting. Respectfully, please do not wear black or white to this event.

Reception

About

The reception is a celebration of the newly married couple — a night to relax, toast, and dance after the ceremony. Expect heartfelt speeches, high energy, and a packed dance floor.

Dress

Indian eveningwear or Western formal attire are both welcome. Think rich colors and statement accessories. This is an outdoor event, heaters will be present and ladies should plan to bring shawls and/or light jackets for the evening. Again, no stilettos, this is a wild agave sustainable sanctuary.

Transportation

Transportation will be provided to and from the venue throughout the evening. Details on pickup locations will be shared closer to the wedding.

Farewell Brunch

About

Join us the morning after the wedding for a relaxed farewell brunch before everyone heads home. This is a chance to unwind, share stories from the weekend, and spend a few final moments together before departures.

Dress

Casual and comfortable. Sunglasses encouraged.

Arriving in Oaxaca

Oaxaca’s airport is small and easy to navigate. Once outside, the surroundings may look simple, but don’t worry — the true charm begins in the Historic Center.

Official taxi to your hotel

  • Exit the airport and go to the official taxi counter.
  • Tell them your hotel name.
  • Pay a fixed rate.
  • You’ll receive a ticket and your assigned driver.
  • Estimated time: 20–25 minutes to Centro. (These transports are shared, so time varies by passengers.)
