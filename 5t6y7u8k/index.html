Travel

One stop away from most major US cities, Oaxaca is easy to reach with a bit of planning.

Flights

Best option: fly directly into Oaxaca International Airport (OAX) or route via Mexico City (MEX) and connect onward. We recommend arriving by Thursday evening to enjoy the full celebration.

Mexico City is one of our favorite big cities! Break up the journey with a stopover if you can. Pro Tip: Centurion lounge at MEX makes a mean carajilo.

Getting around

Most guests will be comfortable using their feet or rideshare/taxis within the city. For events outside town, we’ll provide transportation. For airport transfers .

For rideshare, DiDi Rider is widely used and reliable in Oaxaca. Download the app in advance and set up your account.

Practical notes

Weather

December in Oaxaca is sunny and mild. Expect warm days and cooler evenings-bring a light jacket or shawl.

Altitude

Oaxaca sits at a 5,000 ft in elevation. Take it easy on day one, drink water (not mezcal), and keep snacks handy.

Safety

Oaxaca is generally safe and welcoming. Use normal city awareness and consider rideshare at night for longer journeys.

Kids

Kids are welcome. If you need childcare suggestions, please reach out closer to the event.

Haldi

About

The Haldi ceremony is a joyful pre-wedding ritual where sandalwood and turmeric paste is lovingly applied to the bride and groom by family and friends. Rooted in tradition, Haldi is meant to bless the couple with good luck, ward off negative energy, and spiritually cleanse the body prior to the wedding ceremony. Expect music, sunshine, and a little playful mess.

Dress

Think bright colors and breezy outdoor vibes. Yellow is traditional and encouraged, but any cheerful colors are welcome.

Food

A relaxed spread of morning bites and refreshing drinks will be served, featuring Indian favorites alongside local touches.

Sangeet

About

The Sangeet is a high-energy celebration filled with music, dancing, and dance performances from friends and family. Traditionally held before the wedding, it’s a night to bring both families together to celebrate love, laughter, and a lifetime of memories. Expect lively music, choreographed dances and a packed dance floor.

Dress

Indian attire is encouraged — think lehengas, saris, kurtas, or festive wear. Bold colors and statement accessories are all welcome. Ideas could be bright pinks, greens, blues, oranges, purple, etc.

Food

Enjoy a festive dinner with a mix of Indian cuisine and local flavors in food stations, along with flowing drinks throughout the evening.

Baraat

About

The Baraat is the wedding procession that marks the groom’s arrival to the ceremony. Accompanied by music, dancing, and plenty of energy, the groom’s family and friends parade together to officially kick off the wedding day. This is a high-spirited, outdoor celebration — loud, festive, and full of movement.

Dress

Wear ceremony-ready attire that still allows you to move and dance. Indian formalwear or festive Western outfits are both welcome. The Baraat involves walking, standing, and dancing.

Food

Light refreshments and celebratory drinks will be available after the ceremony. A full meal will follow later, so enjoy a sip, soak in the excitement, and save room for what’s to come.

Ceremony

About

The Hindu wedding ceremony is a sacred commitment and symbolic celebration of love, commitment, and unity. Rooted in ancient traditions of the wedding of Vishnu and Lakshmi, the ceremony is led by a priest through a series of rituals, prayers, and vows. Guests are invited to observe, reflect, and celebrate alongside the couple.

Dress

This is a formal and traditional event. Indian attire is encouraged — saris, lehengas, kurtas are all welcome. Please plan for an outdoor setting.

Food

A traditional Gujarati meal will follow the ceremony.

Reception

About

The reception is a celebration of the newly married couple — a night to relax, toast, and dance after the ceremony. Expect heartfelt speeches, high energy, and a packed dance floor as we bring the wedding weekend to a joyful close.

Dress

This is an elegant, festive affair. Indian eveningwear or elegant Western formal attire are both welcome. Think rich colors, elevated fabrics, and statement accessories.

Food

A seated dinner and celebratory drinks will be served, featuring a thoughtfully curated menu of local Oaxacan cuisine.

Transportation

Transportation will be provided to and from the venue throughout the evening. Details on pickup location will be shared closer to the wedding.

Farewell Brunch

About

Join us the morning after the wedding for a relaxed farewell brunch before everyone heads home. This is a chance to unwind, share stories from the weekend, and spend a few final moments together before departures. Come as you are — no agenda, just good company and lingering celebration.

Dress

Casual and comfortable. Sunglasses encouraged.

Food

A laid-back brunch spread will be served, featuring breakfast favorites, coffee, and fresh fruits.

Arriving in Oaxaca

Oaxaca’s airport is small and easy to navigate. Once outside, the surroundings may look simple, but don’t worry — the true charm begins in the Historic Center.

Official taxi to your hotel

  • Exit the airport and go to the official taxi counter.
  • Tell them your hotel name.
  • Pay a fixed rate.
  • You’ll receive a ticket and your assigned driver.
  • Estimated time: 20–25 minutes to Centro. (These transports are shared, so time varies by passengers.)
